# Facility Inspections for Ambulatory Surgery Centers

## Overview
Texas ASCs are subject to regular inspections by state and federal agencies. Understanding inspection types, schedules, and requirements is essential for compliance.

## Types of Inspections

### State Inspections (DSHS)
- **Initial Inspection**: Required before license issuance
- **Renewal Inspection**: May be required during license renewal
- **Complaint Investigation**: Triggered by patient or staff complaints
- **Follow-up Inspection**: Verifies correction of deficiencies

### Federal Inspections (CMS)
- **Initial Certification Survey**: Required for Medicare participation
- **Validation Survey**: Random surveys to verify state findings
- **Complaint Survey**: Investigation of serious complaints

### Fire Marshal Inspections
- Required annually
- Focuses on fire safety equipment, exits, and evacuation plans

## Inspection Preparation

### 30 Days Before
- Review all policies and procedures
- Verify all equipment maintenance logs are current
- Ensure staff credentials are up to date
- Check medication storage and documentation
- Review emergency equipment and supplies

### 1 Week Before
- Conduct internal walk-through inspection
- Brief all staff on inspection procedures
- Organize all required documents in accessible location
- Verify all signage is current and visible
- Test all emergency equipment

### Day of Inspection
- Designate a staff member to accompany surveyor
- Be cooperative and professional
- Provide requested documents promptly
- Take notes on surveyor comments
- Do not volunteer information beyond what is asked

## Common Inspection Findings
1. Incomplete or missing patient records
2. Expired staff credentials or training certificates
3. Medication storage and documentation issues
4. Infection control lapses
5. Equipment maintenance log gaps
6. Emergency equipment not readily accessible
7. Policy and procedure not followed or documented

## Post-Inspection Process
- Review inspection report carefully within 24 hours
- Develop Plan of Correction for all deficiencies
- Submit Plan of Correction within required timeframe (typically 10 days)
- Implement corrections immediately
- Document all corrective actions
- Prepare for potential follow-up inspection

## Avoiding Deficiencies
- Conduct monthly internal audits
- Maintain comprehensive documentation
- Ensure all staff are properly trained and credentialed
- Keep all policies and procedures current
- Address issues immediately when identified
