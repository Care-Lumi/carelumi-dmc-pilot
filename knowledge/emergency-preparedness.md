# Emergency Preparedness for ASCs

## Overview
Texas ASCs must have comprehensive emergency preparedness plans to respond to various emergencies and ensure patient and staff safety.

## Required Emergency Plan Components

### Risk Assessment
- Identify potential emergencies specific to your location
- Natural disasters (hurricanes, tornadoes, floods)
- Facility emergencies (fire, power outage, equipment failure)
- Medical emergencies (cardiac arrest, anaphylaxis)
- Security threats (active shooter, bomb threat)

### Emergency Policies and Procedures
Must include written procedures for:
- Fire and evacuation
- Medical emergencies
- Natural disasters
- Utility failures
- Security threats
- Communication systems failure
- Missing patient or infant

### Communication Plan
- Primary and backup communication methods
- Contact list for all staff
- Emergency notification system
- Media relations protocol
- Patient/family notification procedures

### Evacuation Plan
- Evacuation routes clearly marked
- Assembly points identified
- Patient tracking system
- Transportation arrangements
- Shelter-in-place procedures when appropriate

## Emergency Equipment and Supplies

### Required Emergency Equipment
- Emergency call system (code blue)
- Emergency power generator (automatic backup)
- Emergency lighting
- Fire extinguishers and alarms
- Eyewash stations and safety showers
- Oxygen supply with backup
- Suction equipment

### Emergency Medical Supplies
- Crash cart with current medications
- Defibrillator (AED or manual)
- Airway management equipment
- Emergency medications (current and sealed)
- IV supplies and fluids

### Emergency Equipment Maintenance
- Monthly crash cart checks
- Generator testing monthly
- Fire extinguisher inspections annually
- Emergency lighting tests monthly
- Defibrillator checks per manufacturer guidelines

## Staff Training and Drills

### Required Training
- All staff must be trained on emergency procedures
- Training during initial orientation
- Annual refresher training required
- Documentation of all training

### Emergency Drills
- Fire drills at least quarterly
- Evacuation drill annually
- Disaster response drill annually
- Medical emergency scenarios quarterly
- Document all drills with after-action review

## Transfer Agreements
- Written agreements with nearby hospitals
- Clear transfer protocols
- Transportation arrangements
- Communication procedures during transfer
- Annual review of agreements

## Emergency Contact Information
Must maintain current contact list:
- All staff with home and cell numbers
- Local emergency services (911, fire, police)
- Nearby hospitals
- Equipment service vendors
- Utility companies
- Medical staff

## Plan Review and Updates
- Review emergency plan annually
- Update after any emergency event
- Incorporate lessons learned from drills
- Ensure all staff aware of changes
- Document all reviews and updates

## Regulatory Requirements
- CMS Emergency Preparedness Rule compliance
- OSHA emergency action plan requirements
- State and local emergency management coordination
- NFPA 101 Life Safety Code compliance
