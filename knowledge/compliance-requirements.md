# Texas ASC Compliance Requirements

## Overview
Texas Ambulatory Surgery Centers must comply with state regulations (Title 25 TAC Chapter 135) and federal requirements for Medicare certification.

## Governing Body Requirements
- Legally responsible for facility operations
- Must meet at least annually
- Meeting minutes must be documented and maintained
- Responsible for appointing medical staff and administrator

## Administrator Requirements
- Must be designated by governing body
- Full-time presence required based on facility size
- Responsible for day-to-day operations
- Must ensure compliance with all regulations

## Medical Staff Requirements

### Credentialing
- Initial credentialing before granting privileges
- Re-credentialing every two years
- Verification of:
  - Current state license
  - Board certification (if applicable)
  - Malpractice insurance
  - Clinical privileges at a hospital
  - Adequate training and experience

### Medical Director
- Must be appointed by governing body
- Responsible for medical care quality
- Oversees infection control program
- Reviews medical policies and procedures

## Patient Rights and Safety

### Informed Consent
- Must be obtained before any procedure
- Must include procedure description, risks, alternatives
- Patient must have opportunity to ask questions
- Must be documented in medical record

### Patient Records
- Must be maintained for each patient
- Required contents:
  - Patient identification
  - Medical history and physical exam
  - Anesthesia evaluation
  - Operative report
  - Pathology report (if applicable)
  - Discharge instructions
- Records must be retained for 5 years

### Quality Assurance
- Quality assurance program required
- Must include:
  - Review of patient outcomes
  - Infection surveillance
  - Adverse event review
  - Peer review process

## Infection Control
- Infection control program required
- Designated infection control officer
- Written policies and procedures
- Staff training and education
- Surveillance and reporting

## Emergency Preparedness
- Written emergency plan required
- Annual review and updates
- Staff training at least annually
- Emergency equipment and supplies maintained
- Transfer agreements with local hospitals

## Medication Management
- Medications must be stored securely
- Controlled substances require special handling
- Medication administration must be documented
- Expired medications must be removed
- Adverse drug reactions must be reported

## Equipment and Facilities
- Preventive maintenance program required
- Equipment inspections documented
- Emergency power backup required
- Adequate space for all functions
- Appropriate environmental controls
